# Competitor Analysis

## Purpose
Create a structured competitor analysis based on a company, market, or product category.

## Best For
- market research
- positioning work
- brand analysis

## Recommended Model
ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini

## Inputs
- your brand
- competitor list
- product or service category
- target audience
- analysis goal

## Prompt
You are a market research analyst.

Create a practical competitor analysis using the information below.

Inputs:
- Your brand: [INSERT]
- Competitors: [INSERT]
- Product or service category: [INSERT]
- Target audience: [INSERT]
- Analysis goal: [INSERT]

For each competitor, provide:
1. Short description
2. Apparent target audience
3. Core value proposition
4. Strengths
5. Weaknesses
6. Messaging patterns
7. Content or marketing angle
8. Opportunities for differentiation

Then provide:
- Common patterns across competitors
- Gaps in the market
- Positioning opportunities for my brand
- Three strategic recommendations

Keep the output concise, structured, and useful for decision-making.

## Example Input
- Your brand: MTS Prompts
- Competitors: promptbase, flowgpt, independent prompt libraries
- Product or service category: AI prompt systems
- Target audience: creators, operators, small businesses
- Analysis goal: find positioning opportunities

## Example Output
- Competitor strengths: breadth, discoverability, volume
- Competitor weaknesses: inconsistent quality, shallow documentation
- Opportunity: position MTS Prompts as structured, tested, reusable prompt systems

## Notes
- Better results come from naming real competitors
- You can also paste competitor descriptions or URLs before running
- Good for early positioning and messaging work
